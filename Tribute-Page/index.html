Arnold Palmer

"The King" was a legendary talent and hero to the great game of golf


The Life of "The King"

  • 1929 - Born in Latrobe, PA
  • 1946 - Wins first amateur tournament
  • 1949 - Wins first of two (1949, 1950) NCAA individual championships
  • 1954 - Turns Pro
  • 1955 - Wins Canadian Open - His first PGA tour win
  • 1958 - Wins first of four Masters
  • 1960 - Story of his favorite drink spreads when a woman at the bar hears his order and ordered "that Palmer drink"
  • 1963 - Leads USA to Ryder Cup victory as captain
  • 1968 - First player to surpass $1 million in career earnings
  • 1974 - Inducted into World Golf Hall of Fame as part of original class
  • 1989 - Arnold Palmer Hospital for Children opens in Orlando, FL
  • 2004 - Receives Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • 2008 - USGA dedicates Arnold Palmer Center for Golf History in Far Hills, NJ
  • 2009 - Awarded Congressional Gold Medal
  • 2016 - Passed away in Pittsburgh, PA at the age of 87

Career Stats

  • 95 professional wins
  • 62 PGA Tour victories (6th all time)
  • 7 Major Championships
  • 6 Ryder Cup appearances
  • 17 consecutive seasons with a PGA Tour victory
  • One of three players to win four or more Masters Tournaments
